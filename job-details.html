About DRS

DRS is a legal and regulatory consultancy based in the heart of London and Newcastle, that provides contract management services to financial firms, including some of the world’s largest banks and asset managers.

Established in 2009, we have approximately 100 members of staff, operating both on-site and off-site from offices in London and Newcastle. Our market leading team is led by our directors who all have extensive legal and industry expertise within financial services.

Our focus is the global derivatives market. We enable our clients to manage their business critical contracts effectively, assisting in driving both their commercial objectives and legal and regulatory compliance.

We’re looking for someone who is passionate, enthusiastic and excited to be part of our team. We will support you to develop your skills through ongoing training, opportunities to learn and offer an excellent work life balance, in a diverse, collaborative and sociable environment!

You will be based in our Newcastle office and rewarded with a highly competitive salary.

Job Overview

As a member of our team you’ll be involved in documentation projects for major financial services companies, gaining experience of regulatory change and legal document negotiation within the derivatives market.

You’ll be exposed to a wide range of financial institutions, from global banks to hedge funds, with client contact from the outset.

The role presents a rare chance to work in an informal legal environment, with the opportunity to learn and develop. You will be trained and supported by leading industry practitioners, senior lawyers and experienced colleagues, to help you become part of our successful growing company.

Key Responsibilities:

Communicating and building relationships with clients and their counterparties. Reviewing and negotiating legal documentation. Managing and updating negotiation databases, keeping track of external communications and ensuring appropriate escalations within the team.

Requirements

  • Law degree and/or GDL/LPC/LLM - 2:1 Bachelor degree or higher
  • Close attention to detail and strength in organisation and time management
  • Committed, professional and focused team player - coming to work each day ready to achieve your goals.
  • Ability to speak French, German or Spanish to an intermediate or higher level is advantageous.
  • Confident and intelligent communicator, with excellent written and verbal communication skills.

Benefits

We offer traditional perks and place a large focus on creating benefits within our relaxed working environment. We’re a sociable bunch, regularly hosting events, participating in sports teams and frequently visiting our office bar!

Please note, recruitment is ongoing and there may be positions available at later dates. If you would like to be considered, please upload your CV!
